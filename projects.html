Nununu is an interactive installation about music and exploration

My senior project at college.
The aim was to show people a new musical experience, where space, sound and playfulness intertwine. Since music is normally lived in stereo, I used 4 sound channels to create a richer experience. The darkness and light sensors are in place to reward people that really explored and interacted with the work.

http://www.tazel.website
December 2016

The installation is made of a dark room with 4 speakers, each with a LED and a light sensor.

The room is always dark, but some lights can be seen, and music heard.

When a person shines a light on a speaker...

The LED and the music respond.

The software, written in Pure Data.

  • Arduino to hook sensors and LEDs.
  • Audio interface with 4 channel output.
  • Amplifiers for the passive speakers.
  • Computer, the brain.

Nununu came about from my desire to work with something experimental using technology and music related for my senior college project. I used Pure Data for the software that played music and coordinated light and sound effects. Sensors and LEDs were wired into an Arduino board, and audio hardware used was a 4 channel interface. Nununu featured four soundscapes with different moods and interactions. I produced a document of the whole process, which you can download, if you’re into that type of thing.

I also worked on branding the project:

  • Manifestation
  • Music
  • Contact
  • Rhythm
  • Playfulness

andreathomazella.com

The site is very minimal and simple, trying to leave as much room as possible to the artwork. A great deal of emphasis went into making something that looked professional and personal at the same time. Using the artist’s handwriting helps accomplish that, besides making the visitor feel welcomed. We decided to show photos of her workplace in the footer, to help create a sense of proximity, “inside the artist’s mind” type of thing. The photo used as header for the homepage is actually a self portrait, but an abstract one. Because that was unique, it was chosen as a work to derive a color palette from, to be used across the site. Transitions and transparencies were a finishing touch used to convey a polished experience.

I worked with other colleagues in college during the design and wireframe phase of the project, but the current implementation is just me, and I work maintaining the project since. @leticia brand, @nina grillo, @camila holanda, do you guys like this new version?
